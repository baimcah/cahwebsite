>_ README.md

This is my personal user manual — a quick way to understand how I think, work, and collaborate.

01.
Working hours

I intern Monday to Friday, 7 AM to 4 PM. Outside that, I'm usually free from 7 PM to 11 PM — that's my most productive window for personal projects, learning, and async communication. If it's not urgent, send a message and I'll get back to you in the evening.

02.
Work environment

Quiet and focused is where I do my best thinking. I prefer to batch my responses rather than stay constantly available — it helps me stay in flow. That said, I genuinely enjoy working side by side with people, especially when there's a hard problem to solve together.

03.
Communication

Written first, always. It gives me time to actually think before responding. I stay on top of messages during my free hours, but I might be slow to reply mid-afternoon when I'm on-site. For anything complex or nuanced, let's get on a call — just schedule it ahead of time. And please, be direct. I prefer clarity over politeness.

04.
Collaboration

I work well independently, but I genuinely enjoy collaborating — especially with designers and people who care about how things look and feel, not just whether they work. I like getting involved early, before decisions are locked in. That's when collaboration actually makes a difference.

05.
Providing feedback

I keep feedback focused on the work, not the person. I'll usually write it out first — it's clearer that way — and follow up with a conversation if something needs more context. I try to be specific and give reasons, not just opinions. I also genuinely welcome feedback on my own work. Tell me what's not working.

06.
Things I value

Doing things properly, not just getting them done. Teams that ship and learn fast. Transparency — I'd rather know the truth than hear what sounds good. A space where asking questions is normal, not a sign of weakness. And people who take their craft seriously, whatever that craft is.
